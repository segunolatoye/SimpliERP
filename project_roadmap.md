# SimpliERP Implementation Roadmap

To ensure a robust system that correctly enforces the stringent business rules and maintains data integrity, the development of SimpliERP will follow this phased sequence. 

Because modules like Sales, Purchasing, and Manufacturing all depend on Items, Stock, and Accounting, we must build the core dependencies first.

---

## Phase 1: Data Layer & Foundation
Before enforcing any business rules, we must establish the database models and access patterns.
1. **Prisma Initialization**: Set up Prisma ORM to communicate with the Supabase PostgreSQL database.
2. **Core Schema Definition**: Define the fundamental models: `Tenant` (for multi-tenancy), `User`, `Role`, `AccountingPeriod`, `Item`, and `Warehouse`.
3. **Audit Log Schema**: Create the immutable `AuditLog` table to satisfy the strict reporting and compliance rules.
4. **Row-Level Security (RLS)**: Implement database-level separation to ensure absolute data isolation between tenants.

## Phase 2: Authentication & IAM (Identity & Access Management)
1. **Supabase Auth Integration**: Wire up login, session management, and protected routing.
2. **RBAC Implementation**: Implement the Role-Based Access Control system to ensure users can only access modules and perform actions (like approvals) that their assigned roles permit.

## Phase 3: The Inventory Engine (The Core)
Inventory is the central hub of an ERP. You cannot sell, buy, or manufacture without items and stock.
1. **Item Master & Locations**: Create the APIs and UI for managing items, Units of Measure (UOMs), and hierarchical warehouse locations.
2. **Stock Ledger & Movements**: Build the core engine for processing Goods Receipts, Issues, and Transfers. This engine will strictly enforce rules such as preventing negative stock, enforcing lot tracking, and writing immutable ledger entries.

## Phase 4: Procurement & Sales
Once items and stock are established, we can implement the movement of goods in and out of the organization.
1. **Purchasing (Procure-to-Pay)**: Purchase Requisitions -> Purchase Orders -> Goods Receipt Notes -> Supplier Invoices.
2. **Sales (Order-to-Cash)**: Sales Orders -> Delivery Notes -> Customer Invoices.

## Phase 5: Finance & Manufacturing
1. **Finance**: Build the General Ledger, Accounts Receivable (AR), Accounts Payable (AP), and Period Close logic. This will hook into the automated GL entries generated by Inventory, Sales, and Purchasing.
2. **Manufacturing**: Implement the Bill of Materials (BOM), Production Orders, Material Issuance, and Shop Floor operations.

## Phase 6: Reporting & Polish
1. **Dashboards**: Surface key metrics (AR/AP ageing, Stock Valuation, etc.).
2. **Report Generation**: Build the PDF/Excel export pipelines for financial and operational reports.
3. **Performance Optimization**: Ensure queries are optimized and the UI remains highly responsive under load.
